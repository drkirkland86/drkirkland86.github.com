    Design Philosophy

    I design and engineer with the belief that successful innovation requires a concerted process with creativity as a centerpiece, motivation as a driver, and knowledge informing all.

    “Nothing is more dangerous than an idea when it is the only one we have”!   ~Emile Chartier.1868-1951

    I try to define the minimum number of parameters by absorbing the perspective and needs of the client. The worst thing to do during the introduction to a project is to limit the possibile solutions. By applying knowledge of technology, materials capability and physical properties, unique (and possibly great!) solutions arise.

    I increase my chances of finding a good idea by giving myself a lot of diverse ones to evaluate. A rapid series of idea generation sessions begins to tell me where to start asking more questions.

    A breadth to depth approach applies to every milestone of development. Group brainstorming is a blast!-so this is an awesome step. Finally, early and iterative testing/redesign facilitates efficient, tailored solutions.
